## Market Trends Overview (2021-2026)

### 5-Year Market Performance

#### 2021-2022: Post-COVID Boom
- **Median house prices:** Surged 15-25% across Victoria
- **Record low interest rates:** RBA cash rate at 0.10%
- **Remote work trend:** Increased demand for larger homes and regional areas
- **Supply constraints:** Building material shortages and labor issues
- **First Home Buyer activity:** Government incentives drove strong entry-level demand

#### 2022-2023: Interest Rate Rises & Correction
- **Interest rate increases:** RBA raised rates 12 times (4.10% peak in November 2023)
- **Price corrections:** Melbourne median declined 5-8%, regional markets held stronger
- **Rental market tightened:** Vacancy rates dropped below 1% in many areas
- **Rental yields improved:** Making investment properties more attractive
- **Auction clearance rates:** Dropped from 80% to 55-60%

#### 2023-2024: Market Stabilization
- **Prices stabilized:** Melbourne median house price around $950,000-$1,000,000
- **Selective growth:** Outer suburbs and regional centers showed resilience
- **Infrastructure announcements:** Suburban Rail Loop, airport rail driving interest
- **Population growth resumed:** International migration rebounded
- **Rental crisis intensified:** Annual rent increases of 10-15%

#### 2024-2025: Gradual Recovery
- **Interest rates peaked:** RBA began easing cycle mid-2024
- **Price growth resumed:** 3-6% annual growth in most markets
- **First home buyers returned:** Improved affordability in outer suburbs
- **Build-to-rent sector:** Emerged as significant market force
- **Regional Victoria:** Continued strong performance (Geelong, Ballarat, Bendigo)

#### 2025-2026: Current Market (February 2026)
- **Steady growth trajectory:** 5-8% annual growth forecast
- **Interest rates:** Cash rate around 3.25-3.50%
- **Supply improving:** New housing developments increasing
- **Rental yields:** 3.5-5.5% depending on location
- **Infrastructure projects:** Driving demand in growth corridors
- **Demographic shifts:** Young families seeking affordable suburbs with good schools

## Key Investment Factors

### 1. Infrastructure Development
- **Suburban Rail Loop:** Connecting growth corridors (Box Hill, Clayton, Cheltenham)
- **Airport Rail Link:** Sunshine, Keilor, Tullamarine corridor
- **Road upgrades:** North East Link, Western Distributor
- **Public transport:** New train stations, bus rapid transit

### 2. Employment Hubs
- **Decentralized employment:** Growth in suburban business districts
- **Major employers:** Healthcare, education, technology precincts
- **Industrial/logistics:** Outer west and south-east corridors
- **Commercial developments:** Werribee, Dandenong, Frankston, Ringwood

### 3. Demographics & Lifestyle
- **Population growth areas:** Outer suburbs experiencing 2-4% annual growth
- **Affordability:** First home buyers and young families migrating from inner city
- **Schools & amenities:** Quality education driving family demand
- **Community facilities:** Shopping centers, recreation, healthcare

### 4. Supply & Demand Dynamics
- **Low vacancy rates:** Under 2% in most growth areas
- **Limited land supply:** Urban growth boundaries restricting supply
- **Development activity:** New estates in outer suburbs
- **Apartment oversupply:** CBD and inner suburbs (avoid these for capital growth)

### 5. Rental Market Strength
- **Rental demand:** Strong across all price points
- **Yield expectations:** 3.5-5.5% gross rental yield
- **Tenant demographics:** Mix of families, professionals, students
- **Rental affordability:** Key driver of demand in outer suburbs

### 6. Economic Indicators
- **Employment growth:** Stable unemployment around 4%
- **Wage growth:** 3-4% annually
- **Migration:** Victoria attracting interstate and overseas migrants
- **Consumer confidence:** Improving as interest rates stabilize

## Risk Considerations

### Market Risks

1. **Interest Rate Risk**
   - **Current:** RBA cash rate 3.25-3.50%
   - **Risk:** Further rate rises could impact affordability and demand
   - **Mitigation:** Ensure loan serviceability at higher rates (7%+), maintain buffer

2. **Oversupply Risk**
   - **Areas of concern:** New estates with rapid development
   - **Risk:** Excess supply could limit capital growth
   - **Mitigation:** Focus on established or nearly complete estates, avoid areas with excessive land releases

3. **Infrastructure Delivery Risk**
   - **Risk:** Delays or cancellations of announced projects
   - **Mitigation:** Prioritize suburbs with committed/funded projects, don't rely solely on future announcements

4. **Economic Downturn**
   - **Risk:** Recession could impact employment, demand, and prices
   - **Mitigation:** Target suburbs with diverse employment bases, maintain financial reserves

5. **Rental Market Softening**
   - **Risk:** Increased supply (build-to-rent, new apartments) could ease rental crisis
   - **Mitigation:** Choose suburbs with structural housing shortages and ongoing population growth

### Location-Specific Risks

- **Melton South/Wyndham Vale/Tarneit:** Infrastructure lagging population growth
- **Officer/Pakenham/Clyde North:** High levels of new construction (oversupply risk)
- **Sunbury:** At top of budget, limited upside compared to emerging areas
- **All outer suburbs:** Car dependency, commute times may deter some buyers

### Property-Specific Risks

- **New builds:** Builder quality issues, depreciation
- **Older homes:** Maintenance costs, renovation requirements
- **Leasehold land:** Avoid properties on leasehold (community land)
- **Body corporate:** Townhouses/units incur ongoing fees

## Action Plan for Buyers

### Phase 1: Research & Preparation (Weeks 1-2)

1. **Get finance pre-approval** - Establish exact borrowing capacity
2. **Engage buyers agent** - Consider local specialist for shortlisted suburbs
3. **Visit suburbs** - Physically inspect all top 10 suburbs
4. **Review recent sales** - Analyze comparables using Domain/REA
5. **Identify target pockets** - Narrow to specific streets/estates within suburbs

### Phase 2: Active Search (Weeks 3-6)

1. **Set up alerts** - Domain, realestate.com.au for target suburbs
2. **Attend inspections** - Weekend open homes and private inspections
3. **Due diligence** - Building inspections, pest inspections, contract review
4. **Rental appraisals** - Get professional rental estimates for properties of interest
5. **Network with agents** - Build relationships for off-market opportunities

### Phase 3: Purchase & Settlement (Weeks 7-12)

1. **Make offers** - Negotiate on shortlisted properties
2. **Contract exchange** - Engage conveyancer, review contract
3. **Finance approval** - Submit formal application
4. **Pre-settlement inspection** - Verify property condition
5. **Settlement** - Complete purchase

### Phase 4: Property Management (Ongoing)

1. **Engage property manager** - Professional management recommended
2. **Tenant selection** - Quality tenants crucial for success
3. **Maintenance plan** - Budget for repairs and upkeep
4. **Monitor market** - Track capital growth and rental market
5. **Tax optimization** - Work with accountant for deductions
