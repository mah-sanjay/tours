## 🚨 RED FLAGS TO AVOID

❌ **Location Red Flags:**
- Properties near high-voltage power lines
- Homes backing onto busy roads/freeways
- Properties in flood zones (check council overlays)
- Areas with excessive new land releases (oversupply)
- Suburbs with declining population

❌ **Property Red Flags:**
- Leasehold land / community title
- Apartments in high-rise buildings (CBD oversupply)
- Homes on slopes requiring significant retaining walls
- Properties with unapproved extensions
- Houses with obvious structural issues

❌ **Market Red Flags:**
- Stagnant sales data (properties sitting 90+ days)
- Declining rental prices in the area
- High vacancy rates (>3%)
- Dependence on single employer/industry
- No committed infrastructure funding

### 🎯 Negotiation Tips

1. **Off-peak buying** - Feb-March and Nov-Dec typically softer
2. **Private sales** - Less competition than auctions
3. **Days on market** - Properties listed 30+ days (motivated sellers)
4. **Comparables** - Research 3+ recent sales in same street
5. **Building inspection leverage** - Use findings to negotiate price down

### 💼 Tax Optimization

- Depreciation schedules (new builds = higher deductions)
- Negative gearing (offset losses against income)
- Capital gains tax discount (hold 12+ months)
- Claim all expenses: interest, property management, maintenance
- Keep immaculate records

## 🎓 KEY LEARNINGS FROM 2021-2026 MARKET

### What Worked:
✅ Buying in outer suburbs before infrastructure announcements  
✅ Targeting family-friendly areas with good schools  
✅ Prioritizing train line access  
✅ Buying in growth corridors (west, south-east)  
✅ Holding through rate rise cycle (2022-2023)  

### What Didn't Work:
❌ Buying CBD/inner-city apartments (oversupply)  
❌ Chasing past performance without future drivers  
❌ Overextending financially (forced sales in downturn)  
❌ Ignoring infrastructure delivery timelines  
❌ Buying in suburbs with stagnant population
